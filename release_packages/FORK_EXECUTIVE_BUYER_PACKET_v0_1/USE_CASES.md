# Executive Use Cases

## Purpose

This document identifies executive-level use cases for Fork.

These are not deployment claims.

They are candidate areas where recomputable evidence may be valuable.

## Candidate use cases

### 1. AI-assisted legal review

Fork may preserve what was requested, what AI produced, what a reviewer saw, what was modified, what was approved, and what cannot be claimed.

### 2. Compliance review workflows

Fork may preserve evidence around policy review, exception handling, escalation, approval, denial, and later verification.

### 3. Internal audit triage

Fork may preserve the observable basis for AI-assisted classification, prioritization, reviewer action, and follow-up.

### 4. Vendor risk and procurement

Fork may preserve the evidence trail around AI-assisted supplier risk review, due diligence summaries, approvals, exceptions, and remediation tracking.

### 5. Regulated decision support

Fork may preserve the observable evidence boundary around AI-assisted recommendations, human review, approval state, execution state, and later review.

### 6. Security and incident review

Fork may preserve evidence of AI-assisted analysis, routing, review, escalation, and response boundaries without becoming the incident-response control plane.

### 7. Employment, healthcare, finance, and other high-consequence workflows

Fork may support later reconstruction where AI-assisted work affects decisions that may be reviewed, challenged, audited, or reported.

## Use-case boundary

A use case is not a pilot scope.

A use case becomes pilot-suitable only when the workflow, source systems, evidence artifacts, stakeholder owners, security constraints, and non-claims can be defined.
