# Executive Brief

## What Fork is

Fork is a read-only, out-of-band, recomputable evidence layer for AI-assisted workflows.

It preserves bounded evidence records so later reviewers can inspect what was observable and captured when consequential AI-assisted work occurred.

Fork is designed for environments where later reconstruction matters: legal review, compliance, audit, risk, security, vendor governance, procurement, finance, healthcare, employment, internal controls, and other high-consequence workflows.

## The problem Fork addresses

AI-assisted workflows change quickly.

Models update.

Prompts change.

Retrieval sources shift.

Policies evolve.

Vendors alter backend behavior.

Personnel move on.

Authority moves between legal, compliance, risk, audit, security, engineering, operations, and product teams.

A decision may remain documented while the basis for understanding it becomes harder to reconstruct.

Fork addresses that gap by preserving the evidence boundary before institutional drift, vendor drift, or system mutation makes later review fragile.

## The buyer question

The core buyer question is:

If this AI-assisted decision is challenged six months from now, what record will we actually have?

Can the organization show:

- what was requested
- what AI system participated
- what was produced
- what human review occurred
- what policy or authority state was visible
- what was approved, denied, modified, escalated, or executed
- what changed afterward
- whether the preserved record still verifies
- what was not captured
- what cannot be claimed

## What Fork does

Fork preserves the evidence boundary.

It can support later review by preserving:

- observed workflow events
- bounded event records
- evidence packets
- manifests
- checksum and integrity information
- verification results
- explicit non-claims
- evidence-state distinctions such as PASS, FAIL, NOT_CHECKED, PARTIAL, STALE_CONTEXT, OUT_OF_SCOPE, and SOURCE_UNAVAILABLE

## What Fork does not do

Fork does not decide whether a decision was correct.

Fork does not prove that AI output was true.

Fork does not perform audit.

Fork does not make legal judgments.

Fork does not satisfy compliance obligations by itself.

Fork does not become the runtime control plane.

Fork does not replace existing governance, workflow, GRC, legal, audit, security, or AI systems.

## Executive takeaway

Fork is not another AI trust claim.

Fork is evidence integrity infrastructure.

It helps institutions preserve what they may later need to inspect.
