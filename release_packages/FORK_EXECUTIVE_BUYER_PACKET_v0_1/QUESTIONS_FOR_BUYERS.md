# Questions for Executive Buyers

## Purpose

These questions help determine whether an executive buyer conversation should move toward pilot discovery.

## Core questions

1. Which AI-assisted workflows would be difficult to reconstruct six months later?
2. Which decisions or reviews create legal, compliance, audit, risk, security, or reporting exposure?
3. What existing systems record the workflow today?
4. What does the organization currently rely on for later review?
5. What evidence would be needed if the decision were challenged?
6. What evidence is currently missing, mutable, distributed, or vendor-controlled?
7. Who owns legal review?
8. Who owns compliance review?
9. Who owns audit review?
10. Who owns risk acceptance?
11. Who owns security review?
12. Who owns remediation or reporting?
13. What would count as a successful pilot discovery outcome?
14. What must Fork not be allowed to become?

## Decision questions

The executive buyer conversation should answer:

- Is there a real reconstruction problem?
- Is there a candidate workflow?
- Are the right stakeholders available?
- Is read-only evidence preservation acceptable?
- Is runtime enforcement out of scope?
- Should the next step be technical validation or pilot discovery?

## Boundary question

The most important buyer question is:

If Fork preserves evidence that something went wrong, who owns the response?

The answer should not be Fork.

The answer should be the appropriate institutional function with authority to act.
