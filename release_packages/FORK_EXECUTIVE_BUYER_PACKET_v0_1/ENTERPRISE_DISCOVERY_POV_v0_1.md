# Enterprise Discovery / Bounded Workflow PoV v0.1

## Buyer summary

Fork can be evaluated through a bounded proof-of-value around one AI-assisted workflow.

The proposed commercial signal is:

`	ext
USD 75,000 per bounded workflow
90 days
`

This is not a binding quote, procurement commitment, production-readiness claim, legal opinion, compliance certification, or security approval.

## What the PoV tests

The PoV tests whether Fork can help preserve a bounded evidence record for a specific AI-assisted workflow so later reviewers can inspect:

- what was requested;
- what AI-assisted output was produced;
- what artifacts were referenced;
- what human review state was declared;
- what checks were performed;
- what was not checked;
- what claims must not be inherited.

## What the PoV does not test

The PoV does not establish that:

- the workflow was correct;
- the AI output was correct;
- the decision was lawful;
- the organization was compliant;
- the workflow was safe;
- the source systems were complete;
- a regulator would accept the record;
- the system is production-ready;
- Fork is deployed in a customer environment;
- procurement has approved Fork.

## Enterprise fit

The strongest first PoV candidates are workflows where AI-assisted output is already entering risk, legal, compliance, audit, procurement, or governance processes and the organization needs better reconstruction of what happened later.

Examples:

- vendor risk review;
- third-party diligence summary;
- cyber triage summary;
- contract-risk review support;
- AI-assisted audit evidence assembly.

## What Fork needs

A bounded PoV typically needs:

- a named workflow;
- representative records or approved synthetic examples;
- AI-assisted outputs or output shape;
- artifact references or digests;
- declared human review state;
- declared non-claims;
- security/data-handling constraints;
- enterprise review questions.

## What Fork returns

A bounded PoV may return:

- workflow boundary map;
- evidence preservation model;
- sample preserved records;
- verification path;
- non-claim boundary;
- reconstruction findings;
- timestamp-pathway recommendation where relevant;
- next-step recommendation.
