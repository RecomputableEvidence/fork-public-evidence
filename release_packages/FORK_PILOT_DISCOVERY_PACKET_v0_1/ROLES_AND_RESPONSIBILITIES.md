# Roles and Responsibilities

## Purpose

This document separates Fork responsibilities from client institutional responsibilities during pilot discovery.

## Fork responsibilities during discovery

Fork may help:

- Define candidate workflow boundaries
- Identify observable evidence surfaces
- Classify artifacts as captured, referenced, unavailable, or non-claim
- Explain recomputable evidence posture
- Explain read-only and out-of-band boundaries
- Map pilot suitability
- Define possible verification outputs
- Draft evidence-boundary materials

## Client responsibilities during discovery

The client must provide or identify:

- Workflow owner
- Technical source-system owner
- Legal or compliance reviewer
- Audit or risk reviewer where relevant
- Security or data-handling reviewer
- Candidate workflows
- Source-system inventory
- Export availability
- Data sensitivity constraints
- Internal approval for pilot scoping

## Responsibilities Fork does not own

Fork does not own:

- Legal determinations
- Compliance determinations
- Audit conclusions
- Risk acceptance
- Security response
- Remediation decisions
- Reporting obligations
- Authority validation
- Workflow approval
- Workflow blocking
- Source-system truth

## Response ownership

If discovery reveals missing evidence, stale context, unavailable sources, failed validation, or out-of-scope claims, the responsible client function must own the response.

Fork may preserve or surface the evidence boundary.

Fork does not become the actor.
