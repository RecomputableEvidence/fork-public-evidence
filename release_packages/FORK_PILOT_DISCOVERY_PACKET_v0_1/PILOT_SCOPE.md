# Pilot Scope

## Purpose

This document defines the initial scope questions for a possible Fork pilot.

A Fork pilot should not begin with a broad enterprise claim.

A Fork pilot should begin with a specific workflow, specific source systems, specific evidence artifacts, specific verification outputs, and explicit non-claims.

## Discovery objective

The objective of discovery is to determine whether a candidate workflow can support bounded, read-only evidence preservation.

Discovery should answer:

1. What workflow is being considered?
2. Why is the workflow consequential?
3. Where does AI assistance enter the workflow?
4. What decisions, recommendations, classifications, summaries, or approvals are affected?
5. What source systems contain relevant observable events?
6. What artifacts can be captured?
7. What artifacts can only be referenced?
8. What artifacts are unavailable?
9. What must be treated as a non-claim?
10. What verification output would be useful to legal, audit, compliance, risk, security, or operations?

## Candidate workflow characteristics

A good candidate workflow usually has:

- Consequential decision support
- AI-assisted generation, classification, summarization, routing, review, or recommendation
- Human review or approval
- Existing source-system events or exports
- Legal, audit, compliance, risk, security, procurement, financial, employment, healthcare, or operational relevance
- A clear later-review problem
- A manageable boundary for a first pilot

## Out-of-scope discovery targets

The first pilot should avoid workflows that require:

- Runtime blocking
- Real-time enforcement
- Full source-system replacement
- Hidden vendor backend reconstruction
- Complete model behavior replay
- Legal admissibility guarantees
- Production-wide deployment
- Unbounded data capture
- Undefined ownership of remediation or reporting

## Discovery outcome

Pilot discovery should produce one of the following outcomes:

1. Suitable for pilot scoping
2. Potentially suitable after narrowing
3. Not suitable for Fork at this stage
4. Requires additional source-system or security review
5. Requires client-specific evidence boundary drafting
