# Fork Preservation Integrity Doctrine v0.1

**Doctrine ID:** `FORK_PRESERVATION_INTEGRITY_DOCTRINE_v0_1`

**Status:** `PROPOSED_FOR_ADMISSION`

**Change class:** `PRE_EXPERIMENT_PRESERVATION_INSTRUMENTATION`

**Historical rewrite authority:** `NONE`

## Purpose

Fork Preservation Integrity maintains the verifiable continuance of an artifact or system state by preserving what existed, what occurred, who acted, under which recorded conditions, and how subsequent observations and amendments relate to the original.

The surface preserves incident states and safe-continuance evidence. It does not determine whether operation may continue, authorize a merge or deployment, certify security, establish intent, or replace institutional incident response, audit, legal, compliance, or risk authority.

## Preservation coordinates

Every admitted incident record identifies, where evidence is available:

- the producing actor or system;
- the observed action;
- the affected artifact and original path;
- exact content identity and integrity digests;
- the temporal coordinates of the observation;
- the recorded environment or unavailable environment fields;
- the claim or representation consumed by a downstream system;
- the observed mismatch or failure condition;
- predecessor, incident, descendant, and amended-path lineage;
- corrective actions and their separate evidence;
- residual conditions that remain unresolved.

An unavailable coordinate remains unavailable. It is not replaced by an inferred value.

## Historical treatment

The original state, incident state, later corrective state, and current projection remain distinct. Later success may explain or supersede an operational path, but it does not rewrite the state that was observed at an earlier temporal closure.

## Claim-consumption boundary

A candidate change may include claims about its own scope, correctness, readiness, or effect. Those claims do not establish repository standing. Admission evidence must be generated by consumer-owned controls operating from a trusted base, together with the applicable human and institutional review.

## Experiment boundary

This doctrine predates baseline execution of Cross-System Claim Handoff v0.1 but postdates its preregistration. Its admission must not modify the experiment hypothesis, corpus, prompt packets, receiver bindings, parameters, run order, classifier, stopping rule, or optimization embargo.

## Output boundary

A preservation checker may report structural conformance to this doctrine. It does not issue a security certification, a universal clean bill of health, an execution authorization, or an experiment result.
