# Fork Enterprise Discovery / Bounded Workflow PoV Packet v0.1

## Purpose

This packet describes a bounded enterprise discovery and proof-of-value path for Fork.

It is intended for enterprise buyers, advisors, design partners, legal/compliance stakeholders, risk leaders, audit leaders, and technical sponsors evaluating whether a specific AI-assisted workflow would benefit from recomputable evidence preservation.

## Commercial signal

A bounded proof-of-value engagement may be scoped at:

`	ext

`

This is a commercial scoping signal, not a binding quote, procurement commitment, master services agreement, statement of work, or guarantee of acceptance.
Final scope, commercial terms, access constraints, data handling, legal terms, and success criteria require mutual written agreement.

## What Fork is in this context

Fork is evidence-boundary infrastructure for AI-assisted workflows.

Fork may preserve a bounded evidence record showing what was requested, what AI-assisted output was produced, what artifacts were referenced, what human review state was declared, what checks were performed, and what was not checked.

Fork does not decide whether the underlying business decision was correct, safe, lawful, compliant, complete, fair, approved, or production-ready.

## Bounded workflow definition

A bounded workflow is a specific, named AI-assisted workflow with an inspectable handoff boundary.

A bounded workflow should have:

- a defined business process;
- a defined AI-assisted event or output;
- defined source artifacts or source references;
- a declared human review state, if applicable;
- a declared evidence boundary;
- a declared non-claim boundary;
- a defined preservation point;
- a defined review question;
- a defined success criterion for the PoV.

A bounded workflow is not an enterprise-wide AI governance program, universal policy engine, live runtime controller, compliance platform, security certification, or legal determination.

## Candidate workflow examples

Suitable first PoV candidates may include:

- vendor risk review;
- third-party diligence summary;
- cyber triage summary;
- contract-risk review support;
- internal policy exception review;
- model evaluation evidence handoff;
- AI-assisted audit evidence assembly.

High-risk regulated decisioning workflows, such as credit, healthcare authorization, employment, insurance, or criminal/legal adjudication, may require additional scoping, legal review, and evidence boundary design before inclusion.

## What the client provides

For a bounded PoV, the enterprise would typically provide:

- one named workflow;
- representative non-production or approved synthetic inputs where possible;
- example AI-assisted outputs or output schema;
- available review metadata;
- available artifact references or digests;
- required data-handling constraints;
- security and access constraints;
- stakeholder review questions;
- out-of-scope claims and prohibited interpretations;
- success criteria.

## What Fork preserves

Within the agreed boundary, Fork may preserve:

- workflow event identity;
- input artifact references or digests;
- AI-assisted output references or digests;
- declared human review state;
- claim and non-claim boundaries;
- checker output;
- package membership;
- receipt or digest relationships;
- verification status;
- PASS, FAIL, or NOT_CHECKED states where emitted by the relevant checker;
- unresolved unknowns where declared.

## What Fork returns

A bounded PoV may return:

- workflow boundary map;
- evidence preservation model;
- sample preserved records;
- verification command path;
- non-claim boundary;
- review packet;
- findings on what can and cannot be reconstructed;
- unresolved evidence gaps;
- recommended production timestamp pathway, where relevant;
- recommended next-step scope if the PoV is successful.

## Deployment posture for a PoV

Fork's preferred PoV posture is:

- read-only;
- out-of-band;
- fail-open;
- no runtime control;
- no policy enforcement;
- no production credential custody unless separately scoped;
- no autonomous decisioning;
- no approval or denial authority;
- no compliance verdict emission;
- no replacement of existing GRC, legal, risk, security, or audit systems.

Depending on enterprise constraints, a PoV may use exported records, synthetic data, approved samples, or a manually transferred bundle rather than direct integration.

## Security posture boundary

This packet does not establish that Fork is secure, production hardened, penetration tested, SOC 2 audited, ISO certified, HIPAA compliant, or approved for customer deployment.

Security review, data-handling terms, access control, retention policy, incident response, and deployment hardening require separate enterprise review.

## Mutual success criteria

A bounded PoV is successful only if the parties can inspect and agree on whether Fork helped answer the bounded reconstruction question.

Example success criteria:

- the workflow boundary was clearly declared;
- captured artifacts were identifiable;
- claim and non-claim boundaries were preserved;
- verification outputs were reproducible;
- NOT_CHECKED states were not silently promoted;
- later reviewers could distinguish what was preserved from what was not proven;
- the enterprise could identify whether the pattern is worth deeper production evaluation.

A successful PoV does not establish production readiness, compliance satisfaction, legal sufficiency, security approval, customer deployment, or procurement approval.

## Out of scope

Unless separately agreed in writing, the PoV does not include:

- production deployment;
- runtime enforcement;
- model monitoring;
- policy automation;
- compliance certification;
- SOC 2, ISO, HIPAA, or regulatory validation;
- security certification;
- penetration testing;
- legal advice;
- audit opinion;
- source-system truth validation;
- AI-output correctness validation;
- enterprise-wide governance coverage;
- customer acceptance;
- procurement approval;
- timestamp validation as a current public claim.

## Suggested first meeting

A first discovery conversation should answer:

- What AI-assisted workflow is being evaluated?
- What institutional decision or review depends on that workflow?
- What evidence exists today?
- What cannot be reconstructed today?
- What would later legal, risk, compliance, or audit reviewers need to inspect?
- What must Fork explicitly not claim?
- What would make a 90 days PoV worth continuing?
