## Initial Evaluation Design: Vendor Risk Review

The first evaluation should compare an AI-assisted vendor risk workflow with and without Fork-style boundary records.

In the baseline condition, reviewers receive the final AI-assisted artifact and any ordinary workflow logs or supporting materials normally available.

In the boundary-record condition, reviewers receive the same materials plus explicit handoff-state records identifying what was claimed, what evidence was referenced, what authority context applied, what was excluded, what remained unresolved, what required revalidation, and what downstream reliance occurred.

The evaluation should measure whether reviewers can reconstruct the basis for reliance more accurately and consistently when boundary records are present.

Primary measures:

* time to reconstruct the reliance basis;
* number of unsupported assumptions made by reviewers;
* agreement between reviewers about what the artifact established;
* agreement between reviewers about what the artifact did not establish;
* ability to distinguish evidence preservation from authorization;
* ability to identify whether reliance was preserved, narrowed, expanded, or newly justified;
* reviewer confidence in claim scope and non-claims.

The hypothesis is strengthened if boundary records reduce ambiguity, improve reviewer agreement, and make unsupported inheritance easier to identify. The hypothesis is weakened if reviewers perform equally well with ordinary artifacts and logs, or if boundary records introduce more confusion than clarity.
