# Public Presence and Buyer Conversion Layer v0.1

## Purpose

This document defines the public-presence and buyer-conversion layer for Fork.

Direction 1 established the bounded public review surface: reviewer entry point, verification commands, non-claim boundary, package index, root-directory boundaries, and quick-start path.

Direction 2 adds the next layer:

- public routing;
- author/contact routing;
- buyer next-step routing;
- timestamp-pathway explanation;
- a worked narrative example;
- discoverability and comprehension support.

This layer does not expand Fork's technical claims.

## Boundary

This layer is not:

- a product-readiness claim;
- a procurement-readiness claim;
- a compliance certification;
- a security certification;
- a legal opinion;
- a statement of customer deployment;
- a pricing commitment;
- a commercial pilot approval;
- a production timestamp-validation claim;
- a source-system completeness claim.

This layer helps a cold reviewer, advisor, design partner, or enterprise buyer understand what Fork is, who maintains it, where to begin, and what a bounded next conversation may involve.

Correct interpretation

A reader may use these materials to understand Fork's public posture, review path, and buyer conversation path.
A reader must not treat these materials as establishing that Fork is deployed, production-ready, legally sufficient, compliant, certified, independently audited, or accepted by a customer.
