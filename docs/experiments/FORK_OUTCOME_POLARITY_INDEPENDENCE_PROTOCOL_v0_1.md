# Fork Outcome-Polarity Independence Protocol v0.1

## Invariant

Favorable and unfavorable outcomes are subject to identical capture, provenance, lineage, and reproducibility requirements.

## Orthogonal axes

`outcome_polarity` describes consequence for the experiment. `evidence_standing` describes evidentiary support. Neither field may mechanically derive the other.

## Typed outcomes

Technical reproduction, failed reproduction, implementation defect, integration defect, specification pressure, experimental-design limitation, retrieval degradation, interpretive disagreement, null result, ambiguous result, professional recognition, attribution permission, collaboration interest, commercial interest, technical validation, institutional determination, and corrected or withdrawn observation remain distinct.

## No retrospective rehabilitation

Later success may explain, correct, or supersede an earlier result through explicit lineage. It may not overwrite the observed historical state or silently import later evidence, checker behavior, runtime behavior, policy, or authority into an earlier temporal closure.

## Non-inferences

Recognition is not validation. Attribution is not endorsement. Collaboration interest is not partnership. Commercial interest is not product-market validation. Structural reproduction is not truth, compliance, approval, or authority.
