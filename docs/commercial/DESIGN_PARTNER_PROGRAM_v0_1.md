# Design Partner Program v0.1

## Purpose

The Design Partner Program is intended for organizations interested in evaluating reconstructable evidence for AI-assisted workflows.

The objective is collaborative learning rather than software deployment.

---

## Candidate workflows

Suitable workflows include:

- vendor risk assessments
- compliance documentation
- internal audit support
- procurement reviews
- leadership briefing preparation
- policy drafting
- AI-assisted research synthesis

---

## Engagement objectives

Together we seek to understand:

- where AI-assisted artifacts become subject to institutional reliance
- what governance context must survive downstream
- which claim boundaries require explicit preservation
- where transition records improve later reconstruction
- which existing governance systems Fork can complement

---

## Typical engagement

Engagements are lightweight and typically involve a small number of working sessions focused on a single representative workflow.

A typical engagement may include:

1. Select a representative AI-assisted workflow with downstream review.
2. Map the workflow's reliance transitions.
3. Generate bounded evidence records using Fork's approach.
4. Evaluate reconstructability with organizational reviewers.
5. Document observations, limitations, and opportunities.

---

## Design partner fit

A strong design partner has:

- an existing or planned AI-assisted workflow
- downstream review, audit, compliance, legal, or risk relevance
- interest in preserving evidence boundaries
- willingness to inspect sample records and provide feedback
- no requirement that Fork replace existing systems

---

## Expected outcomes

A design partner engagement may produce:

- workflow maps
- transition analyses
- evidence-boundary examples
- reviewer feedback
- recommendations for future product direction

The program is exploratory.

It is intended to validate practical workflows, refine governance patterns, and ensure that Fork addresses real operational needs without disrupting existing institutional processes.

Organizations interested in participating should have established AI-assisted workflows where later review, auditability, or evidence preservation is operationally important.
