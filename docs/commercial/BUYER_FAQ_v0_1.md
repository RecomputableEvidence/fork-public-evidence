# Buyer FAQ v0.1

## What is Fork?

Fork is evidence-boundary infrastructure for AI-assisted workflows.

It creates recomputable, tamper-evident records that preserve the governance context surrounding AI-assisted artifacts so later reviewers can independently reconstruct the basis for reliance.

---

## What problem does Fork solve?

Organizations increasingly rely on AI-assisted work products without preserving sufficient context for later review.

Fork preserves information such as:

- workflow context
- evidence references
- claim boundaries
- explicit non-claims
- review transitions
- downstream reliance context
- structural verification status

This helps later reviewers understand what crossed a governance boundary without reconstructing institutional intent from incomplete records.

---

## Does Fork determine whether an AI output is correct?

No.

Fork does not determine truth, safety, legality, regulatory compliance, or organizational approval.

Those decisions remain with the appropriate reviewers and institutional processes.

---

## Is Fork an audit log?

No.

Audit logs record that actions occurred.

Fork preserves what those actions meant in terms of claims, evidence, and reliance boundaries.

---

## Does Fork replace AI governance platforms?

No.

Fork is intended to complement existing governance, security, compliance, and workflow platforms by preserving reconstructable evidence at workflow transitions.

---

## Does Fork control model behavior?

No.

Fork is intentionally fail-open.

It does not block prompts, enforce runtime policy, or approve model execution.

---

## What does structural verification mean?

Structural verification means confirming that a preserved record is complete, internally consistent, and untampered within Fork's defined verification scope.

It does not mean the underlying business claim is true, compliant, safe, approved, or legally sufficient.

---

## Who benefits most from Fork?

Organizations where AI-assisted artifacts influence operational or institutional decisions, including:

- Compliance
- Internal Audit
- Legal
- Risk Management
- Vendor Risk
- Enterprise Architecture
- AI Governance

---

## What stage is Fork in?

Fork is an active research and engineering project with executable specifications, validation tooling, governance simulations, and publicly available proof surfaces.

It is currently seeking design partners to evaluate real-world workflows.
