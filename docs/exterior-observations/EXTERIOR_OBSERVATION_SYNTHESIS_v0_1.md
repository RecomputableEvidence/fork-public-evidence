Question answered: What recurring architectural themes appear across independent exterior observations of Fork?

## Overview

Across six independent exterior observations, several recurring architectural themes appeared. Each observation is preserved with its own scope, interaction type, limitations, and non-endorsement. This synthesis describes what those observations collectively point to; it does not assert that Fork's architecture is therefore complete or correct.

## Recurring Theme A: Evidence boundary separate from authority

Raised independently by:

- EXTERIOR_OBSERVATION_LONG_LIVED_REVALIDATION_v0_1
- EXTERIOR_OBSERVATION_SEMANTIC_BOUNDARY_EVALUATOR_v0_1
- EXTERIOR_OBSERVATION_AGICOMPLY_INTEROP_v0_1
- EXTERIOR_OBSERVATION_EXECUTION_ADMISSION_v0_1

Summary:

> Multiple observers independently described Fork as preserving evidence without transferring authority. The wording differs between observations, but the boundary appears consistently recognized.

## Recurring Theme B: Recomputation as structural reproduction

Raised independently by:

- EXTERIOR_OBSERVATION_SEMANTIC_BOUNDARY_EVALUATOR_v0_1
- EXTERIOR_OBSERVATION_AGICOMPLY_INTEROP_v0_1

Summary:

> Observers consistently treated recomputation as proof that preserved structure reproduced, not that underlying claims became sufficient or compliant.

## Recurring Theme C: Semantic boundary remains an open surface

Raised independently by:

- EXTERIOR_OBSERVATION_SEMANTIC_BOUNDARY_EVALUATOR_v0_1
- EXTERIOR_OBSERVATION_LONG_LIVED_REVALIDATION_v0_1

Summary:

> Several observations converge on the semantic layer: authority-type language can sometimes be paraphrased around the checker, while benign technical language can also trigger it. Observers framed this as an unresolved classification surface rather than a defect silently fixed.

## Recurring Theme D: Upstream admission and downstream procurement adjacency

Raised independently by:

- EXTERIOR_OBSERVATION_AGICOMPLY_INTEROP_v0_1
- EXTERIOR_OBSERVATION_EXECUTION_ADMISSION_v0_1

Summary:

> Observers mapped Fork against neighboring systems: upstream governed admission and execution authority, and downstream procurement / authorization-to-operate / control mapping. Fork itself was consistently described as an evidence-boundary preservation layer between these responsibilities.

## Interpretation boundary

This synthesis describes recurring observations. It does not claim consensus, correctness, or sufficiency. It is itself an exterior, informative artifact, not a normative specification.
