# Exterior Observations

This surface preserves bounded exterior observations about Fork's evidence-boundary architecture. These artifacts are **observations**, not endorsements, reviews, certifications, or authority transfers.

Each observation is recorded as a machine-readable Markdown receipt with explicit metadata:

- identifier
- observer
- date
- interaction_type
- scope_requested
- scope_performed
- execution_performed
- endorsement
- limitations
- classification

The purpose of this corpus is to:

- Preserve what independent observers actually said, within their own scope and limitations.
- Make recurring architectural themes visible without treating them as consensus or proof of correctness.
- Provide a governed evidence surface that can be recomputed, analyzed, and extended over time.

## Purpose

This directory preserves bounded exterior observations of Fork.

These observations are not treated as endorsements, reviews, certifications, approvals, validations, or authority transfers. They are preserved as exterior evidence about how different practitioners interpret Fork's evidence-boundary surface, adjacent responsibilities, pressure points, and failure modes.

The purpose of this corpus is to preserve ambiguity, limitation, partiality, correction, disagreement, and architectural evolution visibly rather than collapse exterior commentary into consensus.

## What this corpus records

Each observation should preserve:

- interaction type;
- scope requested;
- scope actually performed;
- whether execution was performed;
- stated limitations;
- non-endorsement;
- architectural significance;
- observed pressure surfaces.

## What this corpus does not establish

This corpus does not establish that Fork is correct, complete, production-ready, compliant, approved, safe, or commercially validated.

Exterior observations may inform Fork's evolution, but they do not become authority for Fork's claims.
