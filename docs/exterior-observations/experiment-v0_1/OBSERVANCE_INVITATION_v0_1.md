# Observance Invitation v0.1

Fork is entering an Exterior Observance Experiment v0.1.

The purpose is not endorsement, promotion, validation, production adoption, certification, approval, consensus, or praise.

The purpose is bounded exterior observation of a controlled evidence-boundary experiment.

Fork explores whether AI-assisted workflow handoffs can preserve what was claimed, what was not claimed, what evidence was referenced, what remained unresolved, and what later reviewers can recompute without allowing preserved structure to become inherited authority.

The current sandbox question is:

> Does the evidence surface continue to preserve distinguishability between structural reproduction, unresolved state, evidentiary sufficiency, authority, and truth-claims under adverse conditions?

Exterior observations are preserved with scope, limitations, interaction type, and non-endorsement. They are treated as evidence about how practitioners interpret the boundary surface, not as authority for Fork's claims.

Useful observations include:

- misunderstandings;
- pressure cases;
- failure modes;
- adjacent-system mappings;
- terminology conflicts;
- claims that appear too broad;
- boundaries that are unclear;
- responsibilities Fork should not absorb.

No endorsement is requested or implied.
