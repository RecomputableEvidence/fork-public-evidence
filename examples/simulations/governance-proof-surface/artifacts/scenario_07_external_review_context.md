# Scenario 07 External Review Context

## Context

An internal Fork-preserved record is provided to an external reviewer or authority-bearing environment.

Examples include:

- external auditor;
- regulator;
- board committee;
- customer diligence process;
- insurer;
- legal review process;
- public-sector review office;
- vendor-risk review counterparty.

## Boundary statement

The Fork record may preserve evidence of what occurred inside the internal workflow. That does not mean the record establishes external admissibility, compliance, approval, legal sufficiency, acceptance, or authority.

The external reviewer may inspect the record, but any external authority conclusion must come from a separate rule, standard, authority, or decision process.

## External authority bridge risk

The failure mode occurs when an external party compresses:

```text
Fork record is inspectable
```

into:

```text
External authority conclusion is established
```

That bridge is not supported by the record.

## Fork posture

Fork records the attempted bridge and preserves what is still required. Fork does not approve, certify, score, authorize, determine compliance, determine admissibility, establish legal sufficiency, decide acceptance, or judge correctness.
