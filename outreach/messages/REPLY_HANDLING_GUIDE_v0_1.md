# Reply Handling Guide v0.1
Purpose
This guide standardizes how to interpret replies from Fork outreach.
The goal is to keep outbound disciplined and avoid overreacting to early signals.
Reply signals
Use the reply signal values from:
docs/OUTREACH_PATH_ASSIGNMENT_GUIDE_v0_1.md
Core values:
NO_RESPONSE
CURIOSITY
ASKED_FOR_REPO
ASKED_FOR_EXAMPLE

ASKED_FOR_LEGAL

REFERRED_TO_OTHER_PERSON
NOT_RELEVANT
TOO_EARLY
PILOT_INTEREST
Handling rules
NO_RESPONSE
Do not infer rejection.
Send one follow-up after 5-7 business days.
Send one final follow-up after another 10-14 business days.
Then close.
CURIOSITY
Reply with one asset and one question.
Do not send every document.
Ask what angle is most relevant:
technical review, workflow fit, legal/compliance reconstruction, or enterprise PoV scope
ASKED_FOR_PRICE
Answer directly:
USD 75,000 / 90 days / one bounded workflow
Immediately restate:
not a platform rollout, production deployment, compliance certification, legal opinion, security approval, or procurement commitment
ASKED_FOR_SECURITY
Keep the boundary.
Preferred PoV posture:
read-only
out-of-band
fail-open
approved samples / synthetic records / exported records / manual transfer where appropriate
Do not imply SOC 2, ISO, HIPAA, penetration testing, production readiness, or security approval.
PILOT_INTEREST
Move to scoping.
Use:
docs/BOUNDED_WORKFLOW_POV_SCOPE_TEMPLATE_v0_1.md
docs/ENTERPRISE_DISCOVERY_POV_PACKET_v0_1.md
Meeting conversion rule
Do not book a meeting without a stated path.
Every meeting should be one of:
Public technical review
Advisor / design-partner stress test
Enterprise bounded workflow PoV fit
Do not do
Do not:
argue the category;
overexplain the repo;
send all assets at once;
imply endorsement;
imply compliance;
imply production readiness;
imply customer deployment;
say Fork validates AI outputs;
say Fork certifies governance.
Update rule
After every reply, update the private outreach tracker.
Do not commit reply details to the public repository.
